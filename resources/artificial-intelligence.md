# Artificial Intelligence

## EU AI Regulation

- [Regulation (EU) 2024/1689 - Artificial Intelligence Act](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/HTML/?uri=OJ:L_202401689)
  Brief description: The EU AI Act establishes a risk-based regulatory framework for AI systems, including prohibited practices, obligations for high-risk systems, transparency duties, and governance requirements.
  Topic: AI compliance, risk classification, governance audits

## AI And Data Protection Guidance

- [EDPB Guidelines 05/2022 on the use of facial recognition technology in the area of law enforcement](https://www.edpb.europa.eu/our-work-tools/our-documents/guidelines/guidelines-052022-use-facial-recognition-technology-area_en)
  Brief description: Guidance on the privacy and biometric risks of facial recognition technology, with a focus on necessity, proportionality, and safeguards.
  Topic: Biometrics, facial recognition, AI oversight

- [CNIL Practice Guide for the Security of Personal Data: 2024 edition](https://www.cnil.fr/en/practice-guide-security-personal-data-2024-edition)
  Brief description: The 2024 CNIL security guide includes new material on artificial intelligence and can help teams assess technical and organizational safeguards around AI-related processing.
  Topic: AI security, GDPR security measures, compliance controls

## AI, Risk, And Audit Readiness

- [NIST SP 800-53 Rev. 5, Security and Privacy Controls for Information Systems and Organizations](https://csrc.nist.gov/pubs/sp/800/53/r5/upd1/final)
  Brief description: While not AI-specific, this NIST framework is useful for documenting security, privacy, governance, and assessment controls around AI systems and supporting infrastructure.
  Topic: AI governance, controls mapping, audit readiness

- [EDPB DPIA Template](https://www.edpb.europa.eu/our-work-tools/documents/public-consultations/2026/edpb-dpia-template_en)
  Brief description: A structured DPIA template that can support risk assessments for AI use cases involving personal data, especially where high-risk or intrusive processing is involved.
  Topic: AI risk assessment, DPIA, accountability
